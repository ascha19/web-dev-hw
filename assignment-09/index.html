That Cup Pattern; You Know the One

Written by Big Oof Corp. | Posted on June 9, 2020

Origins

According to Wikipedia, which is obviously the most reliable site ever, the pattern in question was never cup-central, and is called the Jazz design, otherwise known as the Solo Jazz Pattern by the prestiged Know Your Meme. This pattern consists of a wide teal stroke in a crayon-scribble style with an thinner, overlapping magenta stroke in the same style; where these strokes overlap, their combined colors make a dark purple color.

The design was created by the Sweetheart Cup Company and was later acquired by the Solo Cup Company. Very little was known about the origins of the design before the design rose to popularity in 2015 and a Reddit thread looked into it. Gina Ekiss, of Sweetheart's art department, is known to be the creator.

Noteriety

The Solo Jazz pattern has become popularized over recent years for radiating Big 90s Energy (B9E) and it's ability to raise nostaligia levels to serotonin-inducing heights. Ultimately, the pattern has been memed and gained a meme following, and can be found on multiple products--not just cups--and within various media.
